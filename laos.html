01

Location

Located in the heart of Southeast Asia, Laos shares borders with Thailand, Myanmar, China, Vietnam, and Cambodia. It is the only landlocked country in the region, with the Mekong River serving as its primary lifeblood.

04

Get In & Out

As a landlinked nation, entry is easier than ever. Most travelers arrive via the Lao-China Railway from Kunming (China) or Nong Khai (Thailand), or through international airports in Vientiane, Luang Prabang, Savannakhet, and Pakse. Popular land crossings also include the Thai-Lao Friendship Bridges and Cambodia (Nong Nok Khiene).

05

Language

The official language is Lao, closely related to Thai. While French was historical, English is now more common in tourist areas. In the North, Mandarin and ethnic minority languages are also widely spoken.

06

07

What to Eat

Don't miss Larb (the national dish‚Äîa spicy meat salad) and Sticky Rice served in traditional bamboo baskets. Beer Lao is the legendary national drink. French influence remains in the excellent baguettes and pastries found everywhere.

08

Getting Around

The Lao-China Railway (LCR) has revolutionized travel, connecting Vientiane to Luang Prabang in just 2 hours. For a classic experience, slow boats on the Mekong remain popular. Note: While the railway is fast, road travel via bus remains scenic but slow (avg 60km/h).

09

Internet & Connectivity

4G/5G is now widespread in cities. For the best experience, buy a local SIM or eSIM (Unitel or Lao Telecom) upon arrival. Reliable 5G service is now live in over 59 districts including Luang Prabang and Vientiane.

10

Electricity

230V | 50Hz

Outlets typically accept two-prong round or flat plugs. Universal travel adapters are recommended for multi-country trips.

11

Visa & Digital Form

Most travelers can obtain a 30-day E-visa online or a Visa on Arrival ($30-$50).
Mandatory for 2026: All travelers must complete the Lao Digital Immigration Form (LDIF) before entry, replacing the old paper cards.

13

Safety & UXO

Laos is very safe with low crime. However, be mindful of unexploded ordnance (UXO) in rural areas‚Äîalways follow warning signs and stay on marked paths.

14

Where to Stay

Options range from luxury boutique riverside hotels in Luang Prabang to authentic village homestays. Guesthouses are affordable and offer great local interaction.

15

Transnational Issues

Discussions continue regarding the demarcation of borders and islands in the Mekong. Environmental concerns regarding dam construction on the mainstream Mekong are also a focus for the Mekong River Commission members.

The New Digital Entry Requirement (LDIF)

üìù Mandatory Step-by-Step for 2026

Paper cards are gone. Follow these steps for a smooth entry:

  • 1. Register: Visit immigration.gov.la within 3 days before arrival.
  • 2. The QR Code: After submitting, save a screenshot or print the QR Code you receive. Each traveler needs their own code.

  • 4. Don't Forget: You must also complete a digital Departure Form before leaving the country.
02

Rail Travel: The Lao-China Railway (LCR)

The railway is now the most popular way to travel across Laos, connecting Vientiane to Luang Prabang in just 2 hours through breathtaking karst scenery.

  • Tickets open: 3 days in advance (domestic) / 14 days (international, e.g. Vientiane ‚Üí Kunming)
  • Official App: Download "LCR Ticket" on Google Play / App Store. Requires a local Lao, Thai, or Chinese phone number to register.
  • Security tip: Swiss Army knives, large aerosols (sunscreen/shaving cream), and certain medicines may be confiscated. Arrive at the station 1 hour early.

üìú Rich Civilizational History

Rooted in the ancient Kingdom of Lan Xang (established 14th century), Laos has a heritage that predates Angkor Wat. The spiritual connection with India brought Buddhism to the region in the 2nd Century B.C., leading to the creation of the sacred That Luang Stupa and the Shiva temple at Wat Phou. After a period of French Indochina rule, Laos gained independence in 1949 and has evolved into a key member of ASEAN and the WTO.
